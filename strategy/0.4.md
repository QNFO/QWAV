## Executive Summary

**Recommendation: Do NOT form an entity yet.** A sole proprietorship is sufficient for QWAV's current stage (SBIR Phase I applications, Zenodo publishing, outreach). Form a Pennsylvania single-member LLC only when ONE of these triggers fires: (1) SBIR Phase I is awarded, (2) ARPA-E application is being prepared, or (3) liability exposure exceeds personal risk tolerance. Cost to form: $125 (PA filing fee) + EIN (free). Timeline: 1-3 weeks. Annual maintenance: ~$70 (PA decennial report, prorated).

**The core QWAV constraint applies here too:** Don't build infrastructure before you need it. Entity formation is infrastructure. SBIR eligibility does NOT require an LLC — sole proprietorships qualify. Wait until you have a grant to receive before creating a bank account to receive it in.

## 2. Entity Types Compared

### 2.1 Sole Proprietorship (Current State)

| Factor | Detail |
|:-------|:-------|
| **Formation** | Automatic — you're already one. No filing, no registration. |
| **Cost** | $0 |
| **Taxation** | Pass-through (Schedule C on personal return) |
| **Liability** | None — personal assets exposed |
| **SBIR eligibility** | ✅ Yes — "sole proprietorship" explicitly listed as eligible |
| **ARPA-E eligibility** | ✅ Yes — same as SBIR |
| **Banking** | Can use personal bank account (not recommended for grants) |
| **EIN** | Use SSN or get free EIN from IRS |

**Pros:** Zero cost, zero paperwork, already active.
**Cons:** No liability protection. If QWAV were ever sued (extremely unlikely at current stage — no revenue, no customers, no employees, no physical products), personal assets could be at risk. Grant-receiving entities typically need a separate bank account (which requires an EIN, which is free and takes 10 minutes online).

### 2.2 Single-Member LLC (Pennsylvania)

| Factor | Detail |
|:-------|:-------|
| **Formation** | File Certificate of Organization with PA Dept. of State |
| **Cost to form** | $125 (PA filing fee) + optional registered agent ($50-$150/yr) |
| **EIN** | Free from IRS (online, same day) |
| **Operating agreement** | Recommended but not required. Templates available for $0-$100. |
| **Taxation** | Pass-through (same as sole prop — file Schedule C). No separate tax return required for single-member LLC. |
| **Liability** | ✅ Personal assets protected from business liabilities (with proper separation) |
| **SBIR eligibility** | ✅ Yes |
| **ARPA-E eligibility** | ✅ Yes |
| **Annual maintenance** | PA decennial report ($70 every 10 years). No annual franchise tax (unlike CA, DE, NY). |
| **Registered agent** | You can serve as your own registered agent in PA (must have PA physical address) |
| **Foreign qualification** | Only needed if registering in DE first. Skip this — form directly in PA. |

**Total first-year cost:** $125 (filing) + $0 (self-serve as registered agent) + $0 (EIN) = **$125**
**Annual ongoing cost:** ~$7/year (prorated decennial report) = functionally **$0/year**

**Pennsylvania is one of the cheapest states for LLC formation.** No annual franchise tax, minimal reporting requirements, and self-representation allowed. This is ideal for a solo founder with $0 raised.

### 2.3 C-Corporation (Delaware or Pennsylvania)

| Factor | Detail |
|:-------|:-------|
| **Formation** | File Certificate of Incorporation, adopt bylaws, issue stock |
| **Cost to form** | $500-$1,500 (filing + attorney) |
| **Taxation** | Double taxation — corporate income tax + dividend tax. Bad for a company with no revenue. |
| **Annual maintenance** | DE: $300+ franchise tax + registered agent ($100-$300/yr). PA: corporate tax returns required. |
| **Investor preference** | VCs strongly prefer Delaware C-Corps. But QWAV's strategy explicitly rejects the VC path. |
| **SBIR eligibility** | ✅ Yes |
| **Complexity** | Board of directors, shareholder meetings, stock issuance, cap table management |

**Verdict: NOT recommended for QWAV.** A C-Corp is optimized for raising venture capital and issuing equity to employees — neither of which is on the QWAV path. The double taxation and annual maintenance costs are pure overhead with no benefit for a solo founder.

### 2.4 S-Corporation

An S-Corp is a tax election (not a separate entity type). An LLC or corporation can elect S-Corp status for tax purposes. S-Corp taxation can reduce self-employment tax but requires payroll processing, reasonable salary determination, and quarterly filings. 

**Verdict: NOT recommended at current stage.** S-Corp election makes sense when net income exceeds ~$60K-$80K/year (the point where self-employment tax savings exceed payroll costs). QWAV has $0 revenue. Revisit if grants exceed $80K/year.

## 4. The Pennsylvania LLC Path (Step-by-Step, If Triggered)

If one of the triggers fires (grant awarded, application requires it, liability concern), here's exactly what to do:

### Step 1: Choose a Name
- "QWAV LLC" or "QWAV Ultrametric Computing LLC" or similar
- Check availability: [PA Dept. of State business search](https://www.corporations.pa.gov/)
- No trademark filing needed at this stage

### Step 2: File Certificate of Organization
- Form: DSS-15-5821 (Certificate of Organization — Domestic Limited Liability Company)
- File online: [PA Business One-Stop Shop](https://www.business.pa.gov/)
- Filing fee: **$125** (as of 2026 — verify before filing)
- Processing: 1-3 weeks standard, expedited options available

### Step 3: Get EIN from IRS
- Free, online, same day: [IRS EIN Assistant](https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online)
- Takes ~10 minutes
- No SSN disclosure to third parties — EIN is the business identifier

### Step 4: Open Business Bank Account
- Requires: EIN, Certificate of Organization, personal ID
- Recommended: Mercury (online, startup-friendly), Novo, or local credit union
- Minimum deposit: $0-$100 depending on bank
- **Critical for grants:** Grant funds must be deposited to a business account with proper accounting separation

### Step 5: Register with SAM.gov
- Required for ALL federal grants (SBIR, ARPA-E, etc.)
- Free registration
- Processing: ~2 weeks (do this BEFORE applying for any federal grant)
- Requires: EIN, DUNS number (or UEI — Unique Entity Identifier, which replaced DUNS in 2022)

### Step 6: Optional — Draft Operating Agreement
- Single-member LLC operating agreement templates available free online
- Documents the separation between personal and business assets
- Not filed with the state — kept internally
- Important for liability protection (shows you treat the LLC as a separate entity)

**Total cost: $125** (filing fee) + $0 (EIN) + $0 (registered agent, self-serve) + $0 (bank account) = **$125**
**Total time to operation:** ~4 weeks (2 weeks SAM.gov + 1-3 weeks PA filing + 1 day EIN + 1 day bank)

## 5. Tax Implications

### 5.1 Current (Sole Proprietorship)
- All QWAV-related income reported on Schedule C of personal Form 1040
- Self-employment tax: 15.3% on net earnings (Social Security + Medicare)
- All QWAV-related expenses deductible on Schedule C
- No separate tax return filing

### 5.2 With Single-Member LLC
- **Identical tax treatment to sole proprietorship.** The IRS treats a single-member LLC as a "disregarded entity" — it doesn't exist for tax purposes. You still file Schedule C.
- No additional tax forms. No corporate tax return.
- Same self-employment tax applies.
- The LLC provides legal liability separation WITHOUT tax complexity — this is the key advantage.

### 5.3 Grant Income Taxation
- SBIR/STTR grants are taxable income (unlike some foundation grants to nonprofits)
- Reported as gross receipts on Schedule C
- R&D expenses offset the income
- SBIR Phase I ($50K-$250K) minus expenses = net Schedule C income
- At $100K grant with $20K expenses: ~$80K taxable, ~$12K self-employment tax

## 6. Risks of Staying a Sole Proprietorship

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|:-----|:-----------|:-------|:-----------|
| **Personal liability from QWAV activities** | Very low | High if triggered | QWAV has no customers, no physical products, no employees, no contracts. Liability exposure is near-zero. Computational/theoretical work rarely generates lawsuits. |
| **Granting agency requires LLC** | Low | Medium | SBIR explicitly allows sole props. Verify specific solicitation before applying. If required, form LLC before submission. |
| **Bank won't open account for sole prop** | Low | Low | Most banks accept sole props with EIN. Mercury, Novo are sole-prop-friendly. |
| **Personal credibility with collaborators** | Low-Medium | Low | Some labs may take an LLC more seriously than an individual. Mitigated by the work itself (papers, DOIs) being the primary credibility signal. |
| **Co-mingling personal/business finances** | Medium | Medium | Even without an LLC, open a separate bank account (free) to keep grant funds separate. This is good practice regardless of entity type. |

## 7. Decision Framework

### Don't form an LLC IF:
- No SBIR application submitted within 3 months → wait
- All pending applications accept individuals → no trigger
- $0 revenue, no liability exposure → no urgency

### Form an LLC WHEN (any of these):
| Trigger | Rationale |
|:--------|:----------|
| **SBIR Phase I awarded** | You need a business bank account to receive federal funds. LLC formation + EIN + SAM.gov = ~$125 and 4 weeks. Do it after award, not before. |
| **ARPA-E application being prepared** | ARPA-E's cost-share requirement means you need a formal entity with accounting. But ARPA-E is not realistic at current stage — revisit this trigger. |
| **Collaborator/institution requires it** | If a university or lab says "we can only contract with an LLC," form it then. Don't pre-form for hypotheticals. |
| **Liability exposure increases** | If QWAV starts building physical hardware, hiring contractors, or signing license agreements — the liability calculus changes. Until then, it hasn't. |

### The "No Premature Infrastructure" Principle

This is the same principle behind the QWAV strategy's rejection of VC pitch decks, team-building, and business plans before there's something to build on. **Entity formation is infrastructure.** Don't build infrastructure before you have a use for it. The use case is: receiving grant funds. When that happens, the 4-week formation timeline is fast enough to close before the grant contract is executed.

## 9. Recommendation

**Do nothing now.** Stay a sole proprietorship. The trigger for action is: SBIR Phase I proposal preparation (P9). When that begins, the following 2-week sequence is sufficient:

```
Day 1:     Get EIN from IRS (free, 10 minutes online)
Day 1:     Register on SAM.gov (free, 2-week processing)
Day 1-7:   File PA LLC Certificate of Organization ($125, 1-3 weeks processing)
Day 14:    SAM.gov registration active → can submit federal grant applications
Day 21:    LLC approved → open business bank account
Day 28:    Ready to receive SBIR funds
```

**Total cost: $125**
**Total calendar time: ~4 weeks (parallel processing of LLC + SAM.gov)**

A 4-week formation timeline is faster than the SBIR review cycle (3-6 months from submission to award). You can submit the SBIR application as a sole proprietor and form the LLC while the application is under review — the LLC just needs to exist by the time the contract is signed.
