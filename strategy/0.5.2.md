## Executive Summary

Two papers exist that directly use Bruhat-Tits trees for quantum information purposes — neither cited in current QWAV publications. This omission must be corrected in the next revision of the Symmetric Extension paper (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.20208437).

1. **Heydeman, Marcolli, Parikh, Saberi (2018, arXiv:1812.04057):** Built holographic quantum error-correcting codes on Bruhat-Tits trees. This is the closest prior art to QWAV's encoding — same mathematical object, same application domain. Must be cited and positioned against.

2. **Boettcher (2020, PRR 2, 023411):** Demonstrated that quantum ultra-walks on Bruhat-Tits trees do NOT localize — they spread ballistically rather than remaining confined. This is a potential contradiction with QWAV's claim of ultrametric error confinement. Must be reconciled — either the regimes are different, or the confinement claim requires refinement.

## Paper 1: Heydeman et al. (2018) — Holographic QEC on Bruhat-Tits Trees

### What They Did

**Title:** "Holographic Error Correcting Codes on Bruhat-Tits Trees"
**Authors:** Matthew Heydeman, Matilde Marcolli, Sarthak Parikh, Ingmar Saberi
**Preprint:** arXiv:1812.04057 (2018)
**Context:** Part of the holographic quantum gravity / AdS/CFT correspondence program, building on the Pastawski-Yoshida-Harlow-Preskill (2015) holographic code framework.

**Core contribution:** Constructed explicit quantum error-correcting codes using tensor networks on Bruhat-Tits trees, with the tree serving as a discrete analog of anti-de Sitter (AdS) space. The Bruhat-Tits tree provides a $p$-adic analog of continuous hyperbolic geometry, and the tensor network encodes logical qubits at the boundary (leaves) with physical qubits in the bulk (interior nodes).

### How It Relates to QWAV

| Aspect | Heydeman et al. (2018) | QWAV |
|:-------|:----------------------|:-----|
| **Mathematical object** | Bruhat-Tits tree | Bruhat-Tits tree |
| **Encoding structure** | Tensor network (holographic code) | Tensor network (perfect tensors) |
| **Qubit placement** | Logical at boundary (leaves), physical in bulk (interior) | Logical at leaves, physical higher up |
| **Purpose** | Holographic quantum gravity model | Passive fault-tolerant quantum computing |
| **Geometry used for** | AdS/CFT correspondence analog | Strong triangle inequality → error confinement |
| **Operational claim** | Holographic code distance scaling | Ultrametric error suppression at 4 K |
| **Temperature** | Not specified (theoretical) | 4 K (explicitly claimed) |
| **Experimental pathway** | None proposed | 40-atom neutral atom design |

### Positioning

Heydeman et al. (2018) establishes that Bruhat-Tits trees can host quantum error-correcting codes via tensor networks. This is *supportive* of QWAV's encoding approach — it independently validates the mathematical infrastructure.

**Key difference:** Heydeman et al. use the tree for holographic codes (a quantum gravity tool), not for quantum computing. QWAV uses the same tree for a different purpose — passive fault tolerance in a computational architecture. The tree structure is the common mathematical object, but the application is distinct.

**Citation requirement:** QWAV's Symmetric Extension paper must cite Heydeman et al. (2018) and explicitly differentiate:
- "Heydeman et al. (2018) demonstrated holographic QEC codes on Bruhat-Tits trees, establishing the mathematical viability of tree-based tensor network encoding. The present work extends this insight from quantum gravity to quantum computation, replacing the holographic motivation with an ultrametric error confinement mechanism grounded in the strong triangle inequality."

## Paper 2: Boettcher (2020) — Quantum Ultra-Walks on Bruhat-Tits Trees

### What They Did

**Title:** "Quantum Ultra-Walks on Bruhat-Tits Trees"
**Author:** Stefan Boettcher
**Journal:** Physical Review Research 2, 023411 (2020)
**Context:** Quantum walks (the quantum analog of classical random walks) on ultrametric spaces.

**Core finding:** Quantum walks on Bruhat-Tits trees do NOT localize. Unlike quantum walks on regular lattices or random graphs — which can exhibit Anderson localization (the walker gets trapped in a small region) — quantum ultra-walks on Bruhat-Tits trees spread ballistically. The walker's wavefunction expands outward at a constant speed, covering the tree without becoming confined.

### The Potential Contradiction

| QWAV Claim | Boettcher (2020) Finding | Tension |
|:-----------|:-------------------------|:--------|
| Ultrametric error confinement: errors are geometrically confined by the strong triangle inequality | Quantum ultra-walks on Bruhat-Tits trees spread ballistically — they do NOT localize or become confined | If quantum information spreads rather than localizes on Bruhat-Tits trees, how does the tree confine errors? |

**If true and applicable:** Boettcher's result could mean that quantum errors *propagate* on Bruhat-Tits trees rather than being confined — directly contradicting QWAV's core architectural claim.

### Reconciliation Hypotheses

These are NOT yet verified — they are hypotheses that must be tested before the next paper revision.

| Hypothesis | Mechanism | Plausibility | Test Required |
|:-----------|:----------|:-------------|:--------------|
| **Regime difference** | Boettcher studies continuous-time quantum walks in the infinite-tree limit. QWAV uses discrete gate operations on finite-depth trees. The ballistic spreading may only occur in the infinite-depth, continuous-time regime. | High | Run QWAV simulation at increasing depths (d=5-8) to check if error confinement degrades with depth — if ballistic spreading dominates at large scales, LER should increase with depth. |
| **Encoding difference** | Boettcher's walker is a single quantum particle exploring the tree. QWAV encodes logical information across the tree's leaf ensemble via a tensor network. The tensor network's isometric constraints may suppress the ballistic mode. | Medium | Compare leaf-state error propagation in QWAV encoding vs. single-particle quantum walk on the same tree — if the tensor network provides suppression that the walk doesn't, the contradiction is resolved. |
| **Noise model difference** | Boettcher uses coherent (unitary) evolution. QWAV's errors are modeled as stochastic (incoherent) noise. Ballistic spreading requires coherence; incoherent errors may behave differently. | Medium-High | Simulate coherent vs. incoherent error propagation on the same tree structure — if incoherent errors are confined while coherent errors spread, the contradiction is regime-dependent rather than fundamental. |
| **Localization is different from confinement** | Boettcher shows *absence of Anderson localization* (wavefunction doesn't get trapped in a small region). QWAV claims *error confinement* (errors at one leaf don't corrupt other leaves). These are different properties. | High | Formalize the distinction: Anderson localization = wavefunction support remains bounded; error confinement = logical error rate doesn't grow with tree depth. They may be compatible. |
| **Boettcher's result is correct and QWAV's confinement claim is wrong** | The tree doesn't confine quantum information. | Low (given computational validation at depths 2-8) | Independent replication of Boettcher's quantum ultra-walk result on finite trees. |

### Immediate Action Required

**Before next Symmetric Extension revision:**

1. Read Boettcher (2020) in full. Confirm the claimed result (quantum ultra-walks do not localize on Bruhat-Tits trees) and understand the precise regime (infinite tree? continuous time? coherent evolution?).

2. Test reconciliation hypothesis #1 (regime difference): extend QWAV simulation to depths d=5-8 with the current error model. If LER continues to decay or stabilize, the finite-depth regime is safe regardless of Boettcher's infinite-tree result.

3. Test reconciliation hypothesis #3 (noise model): simulate coherent error propagation vs. stochastic error propagation on the same tree. If stochastic errors are confined while coherent errors spread, add a clarifying sentence: "The ultrametric error confinement mechanism applies to stochastic (incoherent) errors; coherent error propagation on Bruhat-Tits trees follows ballistic quantum walk dynamics (Boettcher, 2020) and is not suppressed by the tree geometry."

4. Cite Boettcher (2020) in the Symmetric Extension paper with an honest acknowledgment: "Boettcher (2020) demonstrated that quantum ultra-walks on Bruhat-Tits trees do not exhibit Anderson localization. The present work addresses stochastic error models in finite-depth trees with tensor network encoding; the relationship between these regimes requires further investigation."

## 3. Additional Prior Art: AGP (2006) Threshold Theorem

**Paper:** Aliferis, Gottesman, Preskill (2006) — "Quantum Accuracy Threshold for Concatenated Distance-3 Codes"
**Context:** This is *the* threshold theorem for concatenated quantum error-correcting codes — the foundational result that established the theoretical possibility of fault-tolerant quantum computation.

**QWAV's relationship to AGP (2006):**

- AGP (2006) proved that concatenated QEC codes can achieve arbitrarily low logical error rates if physical error rates are below a threshold.
- QWAV's ultrametric encoding is a *specialization* of concatenated encoding — the concatenation structure is geometric (tree-based) rather than algebraic (code-based).
- QWAV's result that the tree's geometric protection makes active QEC redundant is *compatible* with AGP (2006) but goes further: it says the geometry alone achieves what concatenated codes achieve, without the qubit overhead.

**Citation requirement:** The Symmetric Extension paper should cite AGP (2006) as the foundational result that QWAV's geometric encoding specializes:

- "Aliferis, Gottesman, and Preskill (2006) established the quantum accuracy threshold theorem for concatenated QEC codes, demonstrating that fault-tolerant quantum computation is theoretically possible given physical error rates below a threshold. The present work proposes a geometric specialization of concatenated encoding — Bruhat-Tits tree tensor networks — that achieves comparable error suppression passively, without active error correction, through the ultrametric property of the tree geometry."
