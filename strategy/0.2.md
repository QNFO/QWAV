## Part 2: Comprehensive Provisional Design

### 2.1 Strategic Rationale: Why ONE Unified Patent

The existing 0.1.md outline covers only the quantum hardware layer. But the QWAV thesis is inherently cross-domain — the Bruhat-Tits tree is simultaneously a quantum hardware architecture, an error correction framework, AND an AI knowledge representation structure.

**Separate filings are weaker because:**

1. **Claim fragmentation:** A competitor could invent around the hardware patent by implementing the software differently, or vice versa.

2. **The ultrametric property is the common thread:** The strong triangle inequality enables passive fault tolerance in quantum hardware AND glass-box explainability in AI AND efficient error correction in software. Splitting these into separate patents dilutes the core inventive concept.

3. **Cost efficiency:** One $325 provisional covers all three domains, vs. $975 for three separate provisionals.

4. **Patent moat:** A unified patent with claims spanning hardware, software, AND AI methods creates a broader exclusion zone — competitors must avoid all three domains to avoid infringement.

5. **The disclosure synergy:** The specification benefits from describing all three domains together — the Bruhat-Tits tree's mathematical properties apply identically to quantum, error correction, and AI. One mathematical framework, three applications.

**Single comprehensive provisional: $325 vs. three separate: $975. Both have identical 0% conversion probability until funded.**

### 2.2 Three Integrated Domains

#### Domain A: Quantum Fault-Tolerant Hardware

**Core innovation:** Physical implementation of Bruhat-Tits tree encoding as a quantum processor architecture.

**Key elements:**
- Bruhat-Tits tree $\mathcal{T}_p$ truncated at finite depth $D_{\text{max}}$
- Holographic perfect tensor network at tree vertices (each tensor: $p+1$ planar indices + 1 bulk index)
- Logical qubits encoded at coarse nodes (depth $d \leq D_{\text{logical}}$)
- Physical states at fine nodes (depth $d > D_{\text{logical}}$)
- Tree-automorphism gates: logical operations as tree symmetries
- Fractal-multiplexed readout: recursive identical decoding at each level
- Preferred embodiment: $45^\circ$ twisted Bi-2212 bilayer, moiré superlattice as natural Bruhat-Tits structure, 4K operation

**Existing outline coverage:** Claims 1-8 in `strategy/0.1.md` — well-developed, ready to file.

#### Domain B: Error Correction Software Method

**Core innovation:** Software method for encoding, error injection, and error measurement on Bruhat-Tits tree structures that demonstrates passive fault tolerance — the method itself is novel, not just the hardware that runs it.

**Key elements:**
- Method for constructing a truncated Bruhat-Tits tree in software (btree.py)
- Method for encoding quantum states as tree paths with configurable depth hierarchy (encoding.py)
- Method for injecting physical error models (bit-flip, depolarizing, independent $X+Z$) at specified rates (noise.py)
- Method for measuring logical error rate (LER) as a function of tree depth and physical error rate (metrics.py)
- The demonstrated result: LER = 0 at depth $d \geq 3$ for physical error rates up to 40%
- Method for computing the energy barrier: $E_{\text{barrier}}(d) = 2^d$ for $p=2$
- Method for determining thermal stability: $\Gamma \approx 80$ at 4K operation

**Current patent status:** COMPLETELY UNPROTECTED. The paper (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.20134944) discloses these methods publicly — the 12-month patentability clock is ticking.

**What makes this patentable (not just a mathematical algorithm):**
- The encoding method solves a SPECIFIC TECHNICAL PROBLEM: active QEC generates heat that exceeds cryogenic cooling capacity by $20{,}000\times$
- The method achieves a TECHNICAL RESULT: LER = 0 without active QEC cycles
- It is tied to a SPECIFIC PHYSICAL IMPLEMENTATION: the Bruhat-Tits tree encoding on a physical quantum processor
- Per *Alice Corp. v. CLS Bank* (2014): the method is "significantly more" than an abstract idea — it transforms quantum error correction from active to passive by exploiting geometric structure

#### Domain C: AI Methods on Ultrametric Structures (Q-PNA)

**Core innovation:** Methods for training artificial neural networks and processing natural language on Bruhat-Tits tree structures, where the tree geometry provides glass-box explainability.

**Key elements:**

1. **Tree-structured neural network architecture:**
   - Neurons arranged at nodes of a Bruhat-Tits tree
   - Activation propagation follows tree edges (distance measured by $p$-adic absolute value)
   - Hierarchical abstraction: coarse nodes capture high-level features, fine nodes capture details

2. **Ultrametric loss function:**
   - Loss computed using $p$-adic distance rather than Euclidean distance
   - The strong triangle inequality ensures that classification boundaries respect the tree hierarchy
   - Error confinement: classification errors at fine nodes do not propagate to coarse (logical) nodes

3. **Backpropagation in discrete tree space:**
   - Gradient descent adapted to tree adjacency (discrete, not continuous)
   - Weight updates propagate along tree paths, respecting the ultrametric distance metric
   - Automatic differentiation over the tree adjacency matrix

4. **Token calculus for natural language:**
   - Method for encoding natural language tokens as paths in a Bruhat-Tits tree
   - Semantic proximity corresponds to tree distance (shorter path = more similar meaning)
   - Token relationships are ultrametric: if tokens A and B are similar, and B and C are similar, then A and C must be at least as similar

5. **Glass-box explainability:**
   - Method for tracing AI decisions as paths through the Bruhat-Tits tree
   - Each decision corresponds to a specific branch traversal — fully auditable
   - Explainability is GEOMETRIC, not post-hoc statistical — the tree structure IS the explanation
   - Distinguishable from black-box neural networks: every decision path is a human-interpretable sequence of tree branches

6. **Physics-informed AI integration:**
   - Method for combining Bruhat-Tits tree geometry with physical simulation constraints
   - The tree encodes known physics (e.g., semiconductor process variables) as hierarchical constraints
   - AI predictions are constrained to respect the tree hierarchy — physically impossible outputs are geometrically excluded

**Current patent status:** COMPLETELY UNPROTECTED. Q-PNA is undeveloped (P6 in backlog). But the CONCEPT — training on ultrametric structures — is novel and can be claimed in a provisional with reasonable support from the mathematical framework and the quantum analogy.

**What makes this patentable:**
- The method solves a SPECIFIC TECHNICAL PROBLEM: black-box AI systems are unexplainable, creating safety and trust barriers
- The method achieves a TECHNICAL RESULT: glass-box explainability through geometric decision paths
- It is tied to a SPECIFIC STRUCTURE: the Bruhat-Tits tree, which has unique mathematical properties (ultrametric, hierarchical, self-similar)
- The tree is not just a data structure — it's a GEOMETRIC SPACE with properties that enforce explainability

#### METHOD CLAIMS (Error Correction Software) — Claims 9-15

**Claim 9.** A computer-implemented method for fault-tolerant quantum information encoding, comprising:
(a) constructing, by a classical control computer, a truncated Bruhat-Tits tree $\mathcal{T}_p$ having a finite maximum depth $D_{\text{max}}$;
(b) encoding, by the classical control computer, a logical qubit as a state vector associated with a node at a first depth $d_{\text{logical}} < D_{\text{max}}$ of the tree;
(c) encoding, by the classical control computer, physical quantum states as state vectors associated with nodes at depths greater than $d_{\text{logical}}$;
(d) wherein the Bruhat-Tits tree structure provides passive error suppression through the ultrametric strong triangle inequality property, such that errors introduced at fine-depth nodes are geometrically confined to their local branches and do not propagate to corrupt logical qubit information at coarser depths.

**Claim 10.** The method of claim 9, further comprising:
injecting, by the classical control computer, physical noise into the physical quantum states according to a specified error model, the error model comprising at least one of: bit-flip errors, depolarizing errors, or independent $X+Z$ errors;
computing a logical error rate (LER) as a function of tree depth and physical error rate;
and determining a threshold depth $d_{\text{threshold}}$ at which the LER is substantially zero for physical error rates below a critical value.

**Claim 11.** The method of claim 10, wherein the threshold depth $d_{\text{threshold}} = 3$ for physical error rates up to 40% under the bit-flip error model, and wherein the LER at depths $d \geq 3$ is 0.0.

**Claim 12.** The method of claim 9, further comprising:
computing, by the classical control computer, an energy barrier $E_{\text{barrier}}(d)$ associated with flipping the state of a logical qubit encoded at depth $d$, wherein $E_{\text{barrier}}(d) = 2^d$ for a Bruhat-Tits tree with branching factor $p=2$, such that the energy required to corrupt a logical qubit grows exponentially with tree depth.

**Claim 13.** The method of claim 12, further comprising:
determining a thermal stability parameter $\Gamma = E_{\text{barrier}}(d) / k_B T$, where $k_B$ is the Boltzmann constant and $T$ is the operating temperature;
and verifying that $\Gamma \geq 80$ for an operating temperature of approximately 4 Kelvin at tree depth $d = 6$, thereby confirming that thermal fluctuations are insufficient to corrupt logical qubit information.

**Claim 14.** The method of claim 9, wherein the classical control computer executes a sequence of tree-automorphism gates implementing a quantum algorithm, the algorithm comprising at least one of: Shor's factoring algorithm, Grover's search algorithm, or a variational quantum eigensolver, and wherein each gate operation is implemented as an automorphism of the Bruhat-Tits tree that preserves the error-suppression hierarchy.

**Claim 15.** A non-transitory computer-readable medium storing instructions that, when executed by a processor, cause the processor to perform the method of claim 9.

#### METHOD CLAIMS (AI on Ultrametric Structures) — Claims 16-22

**Claim 16.** A computer-implemented method for training an artificial neural network with glass-box explainability, comprising:
(a) constructing a Bruhat-Tits tree $\mathcal{T}_p$ having a plurality of nodes arranged in a hierarchical structure;
(b) arranging a plurality of artificial neurons at nodes of the Bruhat-Tits tree, wherein neurons at coarser tree depths process higher-level abstract features and neurons at finer tree depths process detailed features;
(c) propagating activation signals along edges of the Bruhat-Tits tree, wherein the distance between any two neurons is measured by the $p$-adic absolute value satisfying the strong triangle inequality $d(x,z) \leq \max(d(x,y), d(y,z))$;
(d) computing a loss function using the $p$-adic distance metric, such that classification boundaries respect the tree hierarchy and errors at fine nodes are confined to their local branches without corrupting coarse-node classifications.

**Claim 17.** The method of claim 16, further comprising:
performing backpropagation through the Bruhat-Tits tree by computing gradient updates that propagate along tree adjacency paths, wherein weight updates are constrained to respect the discrete tree topology, and wherein the strong triangle inequality ensures that gradient contributions from distant tree branches do not interfere.

**Claim 18.** The method of claim 16, further comprising:
encoding a plurality of natural language tokens as paths in the Bruhat-Tits tree, wherein a semantic proximity between any two tokens corresponds to a tree distance therebetween, such that tokens with similar meaning have shorter tree paths between them;
and wherein token relationships are ultrametric: for any three tokens A, B, C, if A is semantically proximate to B and B is semantically proximate to C, then A is at least as semantically proximate to C.

**Claim 19.** The method of claim 16, further comprising:
providing glass-box explainability for an AI decision by tracing the decision as a path through the Bruhat-Tits tree from a root node to a leaf node, wherein each branching point along the path represents a human-interpretable classification decision, and wherein the tree structure itself constitutes the explanation without requiring post-hoc statistical analysis.

**Claim 20.** The method of claim 16, further comprising:
integrating physical simulation constraints into the Bruhat-Tits tree structure, wherein known physical variables associated with a process-engineering domain are encoded as hierarchical constraints at specified tree depths;
and constraining the neural network's predictions to respect the tree hierarchy, such that outputs violating known physical constraints are geometrically excluded from the set of reachable leaf nodes.

**Claim 21.** The method of claim 20, wherein the process-engineering domain is semiconductor manufacturing, and wherein the physical variables comprise at least one of: dopant concentration, layer thickness, etch depth, deposition rate, or thermal budget, and wherein the constrained predictions are used to optimize a semiconductor fabrication process.

**Claim 22.** A non-transitory computer-readable medium storing instructions that, when executed by a processor, cause the processor to perform the method of claim 16.

**The $20{,}000\times$ cooling gap argument:**

The claimed invention eliminates active QEC cycles entirely, enabling operation at 4K with 1 W cooling budget — a $20{,}000\times$ improvement in cooling headroom. This is the specific, quantitative technical problem the invention solves.
