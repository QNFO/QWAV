## Why This Approach Works

| Element | Rationale |
|:--------|:----------|
| **Specific reference to his work** | Shows you've read his papers. "Bridging p-adic methods across physics, biology, ML" tells him you understand his unique role. |
| **Zenodo DOI (Version A)** | Tangible proof you ship work, not just ideas. A DOI is a real publication even without peer review. |
| **No overclaim** | Neither version says "I've solved quantum computing." They say "I'm working on" or "exploring." This is honest and prevents the crackpot filter. |
| **Binary questions** | Answerable in 30 seconds. He can reply with two words. Low friction is everything. |
| **Gratitude without groveling** | You thank him for the *work*, not for reading your email. Peer-to-peer, not supplicant-to-gatekeeper. |

## Follow-Up Protocol

- **No response after 2 weeks:** Send ONE brief follow-up. "Just bumping my note from [date]. I know you're busy. If the conference date isn't set yet, I'll keep an eye on the website. Thanks again."
- **Still no response:** Move on. Not every door opens. You've lost nothing.
- **Positive response:** Prepare a one-page abstract centered on the structural connection between ultrametricity and quantum computation. Do NOT write it until you know the specific call for abstracts format.

## Alignment with QWAV Strategy

This is consistent with the **Introvert's Path** (written-only communication). Email is written. No live pitching, no conference schmoozing, no networking events required. If he responds, the next step is also written (abstract submission). This stays entirely within the written-first, substance-first framework.
